Roles

Once every night, the growl of the bear resonates through the village if a wolf is directly beside you. If you are a wolf, the bear may or may not growl every night regardless of the presence of wolves beside you.
Once every night, you may designate a player. The designated target is announced to the entire village the next morning. When tallying the votes for lynching, the total vote count for the designated target is doubled.
Once every night, you may sniff three consecutive players around the village. Your flair, strangely unreliable, only lets you know if there is at least one wolf among them. If you fail to find a wolf, your confidence in your flair is gone. You may never use your ability again.
Once every night, you may choose to heal any one player including yourself. If the healed player was attacked during the night, the attack is negated. No player may be healed two nights in a row.
In the moment before you die, your role is revealed to the village. You may then choose to shoot another player with your rifle and bring them down with you.
In the moment where you are designated to be hung, your role is revealed to the village. Feeling foolish for having thought you capable of such devious crimes, the villagers decide to spare you. However, they lose trust in your capacity to make an informed decision any longer stripping your right to vote for any future decisions.
Once every night, you may curse a player of your choice. The cursed player may not use their ability or vote until the end of the next night.
Once over the course of the game, after a lynching is complete, you may reveal your role and initiate a new lynching.
Once every night, you may compare the aura of two players of your choice. It will be revealed to you if they can win the game together or not.
Once over the course of the game when a player is announced dead, you may opt to reveal your role and replace it with that player’s.
Once every night, you may designate a player. The designated target is announced to the entire village the next morning. When tallying the votes for lynching, the total vote count for the designated target is halved.
Once every night, you may see the true nature of a player of your choice. A vision from a powerful being above shall reveal whether that player is a wolf or not.
Once every night, all remaining siblings meet for a brief silent exchange. Once over the course of the game, they may elect to execute a player provided they unanimously agree to do so.
At the end of every night, you may assume the role of the player of your choice leaving them as a thief instead.
If you are attacked by the wolves, you are announced wounded in the morning. You will die after the lynching of the day. If you were lynched, your role is revealed to the village. The guilt causes the village to cease functioning, henceforth all players lose access to their roles’ abilities.
You brewed two elusive potions to use over the course of the game, one that heals and one that kills but only with a full dose. Absent minded as you are, you don’t remember which flask contains which potion. Once every night, you discover who has been attacked. You may then choose to use potions on players of your choice.
