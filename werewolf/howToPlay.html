How to Play

Setup

Assemble a group of seven to twenty players as well as someone willing to be game master to begin a game of werewolf. Refer to the role and allegiance card distribution chart to select the cards that form each of the two sets based on the number of players. The game master then distributes one role card and one allegiance card face down to each player respectively. Players should discretely glance at the cards they were dealt to discover what character they will be playing this game. There is a total of 192 unique character combinations of role and allegiance a player may obtain!


Gameplay

The game consists of continuously cycling night and day phases always beginning with a night. Players considered dead give their cards to the game master, leave the game, and may spectate the rest of the game from the sidelines. A game ends when no players with opposing win conditions associated with their allegiance remain.


Night:

During the night, all players close their eyes and attempt to be as quiet as possible. The game master directs the flow of the night by calling players with specific roles or allegiances to wake in turn based on the call order provided below. When players are called by the game master, they may open their eyes and make decisions using signs but must remain silent. Once a player’s action is complete, they must close their eyes once again.

In addition to all the abilities associated with roles and allegiances, all werewolves except for a dream wolf wake up together once every night to select one player to attack that night. Once all actions are complete, at the signal of the game master, players wake up. The game master then announces those who have been killed, wounded, designated, or cursed as the result of the actions that took place in the night after which the day phase ensues.


Day:

At the beginning of the first day of the game, players nominate each other or themselves to be the captain of the village. Those nominated may optionally give a speech explaining why they wish to be captain. A captain is then elected by popular vote and given a special item to be distinguishable as captain to all other players (hat, rock, cushion, etc.). The vote of the captain serves as a tie breaker during the lynching process. If at any point the captain dies, the captain may choose who shall be the next captain and give them the special item.

During the core of the day, players discuss and deliberate for a limited time to share information, deceive, and gain insight on the characters of the other players. They must then move on to accusations where they nominate candidates to be lynched. A vote for the lynching is then made, and the winning candidate is considered lynched by the village and dies. Promptly, the following night begins unless the game has ended.

Limits

Avoid:
  • Revealing one of your whole cards unless explicitly stated in the context of an ability associated with a role or allegiance.
  • Revealing any information before the game master announces the results of the night or after the village votes to lynch you.
  • Discussing any information related to the on-going game privately to another player rather than to all other players while you are alive.
  • Discussing information related to the on-going game loud enough for live players to hear after you die.
  • Declaring a mistake was made or ending the game without first confirming with the game master.
